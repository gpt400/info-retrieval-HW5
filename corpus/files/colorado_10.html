MySkills

The job search can be stressful and daunting. Especially when youre wondering: Aside from my degree, what else should I do to land my dream job? What will set me apart, and how can I find that out? If I need additional skills, where can I build them? How do I highlight my accomplishments and skills in a way that will put my resume at the top of the stack?

MySkills helps you navigate all of those questions and more. From helping you understand the top skills that all industries and all roles are hiring for to the exact skills youll need for specific roles and industries, weve got you. Well also help you determine your unique skill set, and showcase exactly why youre a top candidate through every stage of the hiring process. The best part? It only takes a few hours, and Career Services will support you the whole way.

Students

  • Request a job or industry report and learn exactly which skills youll need in order to be competitive in your chosen field. Youll receive a report by email, along with instructions on how to make the most of the data.
  • Build skills through small group projects, job simulations, freelance gigs and more. Our professional experiences team can help.
  • Connect with alumni and set up an informational interview about jobs or industries youre interested in. They love to share their wisdom about their field, common mistakes to avoid and advice on just starting out.

Faculty

  • Wanting to introduce career strategies into your classroom? Request a MySkills presentation. A staff member from Career Services will lead your students through in-class activities, and help them figure out their next steps.
  • You can also introduce your students to career strategies through Career Services Canvas modules that you can import directly into your course. They include activities, discussions and teaching notes. The MySkills module also comes with instructional videos for your students. Request access or attend an information session.
  • Thinking of making a lateral move or going for a promotion or tenure, and youd like a refresher on career-related materials? Perhaps youd like to see a presentation in order to decide if its right for your class. Join us! Wed love to see you!
