Student Research

Student research at a glance

Rochester studentsat the undergraduate, graduate, and even pre-college levelconduct research in the lab and in the field, independently and with industry partners, in our local communities and all over the world. In doing so, they help us better understand and radically reimagine the world while building their academic portfolios.

Printing plant-based pharmaceuticalswithout plants

A group of students developed a 3D-bioprinting system to help replicate chemicals found in plants.

Exploring how the brain processes speech syntax

Sophea Urbi Biswas 24 is a biomedical engineering student. Her hands-on lab experience as an undergraduate is driving new discoveries. Its exciting pursuing questions that have never been asked before, and getting results that have never been found before.

Wired for research
The ethical dilemmas of emergency care

Rachel Whitmoyer 24 is a double major in physics and philosophy, as well as an EMT. Shes combining her passions in her cross-discipline thesis, which emphasizes that high-quality patient care cannot exist without realizing that a patient is a person, and not a problem to be solved.
